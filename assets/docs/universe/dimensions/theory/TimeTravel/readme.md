### 1.  Why macroscopic travel breaks consistency

When you try to carry a whole mass through a time-reversed domain, three fundamental constraints appear:

| Constraint         | What happens                                                                                                                                                                     |

| **Causality**      | A macroscopic object contains billions of entangled particles; reversing all their time arrows would require erasing every causal chain they form with the rest of the universe. |

| **Energy density** | To reach a ‚Äútemporal turning point,‚Äù space-time curvature must approach the Planck or black-hole regime, far beyond any sustainable EMDPL zone.                                  |

So the EMDPL defines a *limit of stability*.  Crossing it with real matter would collapse structure before any temporal inversion could complete.

PT-symmetric systems **mimic** time reversal locally:

* In condensed-matter or metamaterials, they yield *exceptional points* where gain and loss balance perfectly, making energy flow appear reversible.

These are powerful analogies for *temporal symmetry*, but not mechanisms for transporting matter.
